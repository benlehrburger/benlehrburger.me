Recent Papers

Toward Artificial Theory of Mind

I wrote this paper on Artificial Theory of Mind in my Introduction to Cognitive Science (COGS 001) course. Here, I first define what is needed to build an AI with a Theory of Mind. I then look at current approaches to Artificial Theory of Mind development, and offer potential areas for improvement or further investigation.

Self-Driving Black Box: Algorithms and Automation

I wrote this paper in my writing seminar called Charting Self-Driving Cars. In the paper, I look at the black box nature of autonomous vehicle software and consider how lack of understanding surrounding the technology may generate safety concerns, privacy infringement, and inequality.

Similarities Between Language and Music May Inform Text-Generating AI

This is another paper that I wrote in COGS 001. I was curious about the creative abilities of AI, so I compared and contrasted music-making AI with text-generating AI. I found that music-generating AI taps into an emotional component of the human mind that text-generating AI does not, and this may serve to help text-generating AI’s cohesiveness.
